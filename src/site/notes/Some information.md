This is an incomplete mirror of my Obsidian Vault that I use to organise my research; I've ommited the notes that contain gibberish notes to myself or uninteresting reminders. Let me give a run-down of my folders: 'Primary Bibliography' is what it says on the tin, with quotes that I plan on citing/have cited, as well as the 'bodies' folders which categorise the types of embodment (i use this later to work on my graphs). 

A lot of the citations are marked (bold, italics, other markdown functions) in some way which makes sense in my head but isn't explained here since this is, after all, a personal vault—don't worry about it. Same with the secondary bibliography. I'm hoping here that since I only have noted down useful quotes alongsider their page numbers, none of this pings as a copyright infringement (it shouldn't but who knows, really). Terminology contains blank notes used only for backlinking and graph view purposes, do don't expect to find anything in there. 

Formatting is, in general, terribly inconsistent, because this is a workspace and not a wiki: often my priority is speed instead of neatness, especially because there is a lot of data here and some of it is old-ish (from my BA and MA dissertation days) so re-formatting it would be a waste of my time. Besides, I'm working fast: this (and by this I mean this programmed monter-amalgamation of my notes) is not really part of my work but a pet project, so I'm working on it in the mrgins of everything else. 

I'm masking this public not because I think it'll ber a useful tool for anyone else but to show my working methods and the technological assemblagers that inform my discourse. It's chaotic and profoundly amateur (I'm not a programmer, as you can probably tell) but it's a move towards academic and technological transparency, open-access resistance, public making a mess of things, etcetera. If I've been very brave and shown this to any professor, please excuse everything about this.
