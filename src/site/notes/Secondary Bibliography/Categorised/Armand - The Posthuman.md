Armand, Louis. ‘The Posthuman: AI, Dronology, and “Becoming Alien”’. _AI & SOCIETY_, vol. 35, no. 1, Mar. 2020, pp. 257–62, [https://doi.org/10.1007/s00146-018-0872-2](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00146-018-0872-2).

the Anthropocene has come to represent the co-option of a scientifc factography for the thinly disguised resurgence of “ideological science” of the Fukuyamaesque variety (posthistory, posthuman) (Armand 257)

>the dénouement of the Anthropocene and the “ends of man” represents the accomplished purpose of species warfare: dominion, not simply over the world, but over all possible worlds (Armand 257)

the prototype of an ideal proxy by which the “human condition” transcends its worldly embodiment into a cosmic internet-of-things: a distributed architecture of autopoietic, kinetic agency. Thought that moves itself (Armand 258)

>the prestige of what is conserved as species unique is equated with the prestige of species transcendence: the (supposed) “uniquely human” capacity to manufacture universalities—artificial intelligence, cybernetic machines, synthetic DNA, etc. (Armand 258)

Humanistic teleology upon the domain of evolution itself—and thus secure a future against human obsolescence (Armand 258)

>biotechnology as political control—where “political control” means social cybernetics. (Armand 258)

>even in the (always hypothetical) aftermath of humanity, the future will be human. (Armand 258)

anthropomorphism can be understood as a mask of the unpresentable (Armand 259)

Anthropocene as technocapital singularity. Its event-horizon shimmers in a vertigo of nascent infnities—hyperbolic space–time curvatures—entropologies of the impossible. (Armand 260)

Anthropocene in turn dictates that the socalled called life-world is always already co-opted to the work of technical re-production, including its own negative production: the global infation efect of “alienation” (Armand 261)

>The ideological force of the Anthropocene is its seeming confrmation of this totality, as the penultimate step towards understanding, regulating, predicting, controlling the “entire world”—like some cosmic avatar of Panopticism (Armand 262)
