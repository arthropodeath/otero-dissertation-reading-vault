About [[Terminology/Transhumanism\|Transhumanism]], [[Terminology/(Trans)Posthumanism\|(Trans)Posthumanism]]

Transhumanism is the thesis that we can and ought to use technology to alter and improve human biology (Walker 94)

The operative assumption here of course is that intelligence, moods, longevity, and virtues each have deep roots in our biology. By altering biology, transhumanists propose to improve human nature to the point of creating a new genus: posthumans (Walker 94)

posthumans will stand to us in moral and intellectual virtues as we stand to chimps. (Walker 94)

Notice that transhumanism encompasses a moral thesis. Transhumanism does not say that we will create posthumans; rather, it makes a moral claim: we ought to create posthumans. (Walker 95

technology has advanced sufficiently to allow an interspecies experiment: one species conducts the experiment to create a new species. (Walker 97)

The posthuman experiment promises to unite cultural and biological evolution. With changes in biology, more intelligent posthumans will likely be able to accelerate cultural evolution, and cultural evolution should enhance posthuman ability to create new species (Walker 97)

. On the optimistic scenario, those that become posthumans lend a helping hand to the other species on this planet, including those who choose to remain human. The age of posthumanity ushers in a paradise of biblical proportions (Walker 98)

A second dual use problem stems from the fact that the same technology developed for benign world engineering purposes can be easily reapplied to more destructive ends (Walker 101)

Even as I write this, private citizens are using genetic technologies in their basements and their garages with no government oversight. This burgeoning movement is referred to as biohacking. For a few thousand dollars and a small room to work, one can become a biohacker. (Walker 102)
