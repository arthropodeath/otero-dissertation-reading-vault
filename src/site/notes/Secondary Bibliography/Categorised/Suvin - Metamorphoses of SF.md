Suvin, Darko. Metamorphoses of Science Fiction: On the Poetics and History of a Literary Genre. New Haven: Yale University Press, 1979. 

About [[Terminology/Science Fiction Studies\|Science Fiction Studies]], [[Terminology/Utopia\|Utopia]]

The noncanonic, repressed twin of Literature which, for want of another name, one calls Para-literature is (for better or for worse) the literature that is really read—as opposed to most literature taught in schools Suvin vii

the SF production has been predominantly determined and strongly affected by the capitalist market with its alienating and degrading tenden(viii)cies  Suvin vii-viii

SF is a developed oxymoron, a realistic irreality, with humanised nonhumans, this-worldly Otherworlds, and so forth  Suvin viii

A potential cognitive tendency ... is allied to the rise of subversive social classes and their development of more sophisticated productive forces and cognitions. However, an opposed tendency toward mystifying escapism dominates in second-rate SF and shows even in the masters ..., formed as it is by the practical and cognitive limitations of fiction steeped in the alienation of class society and in particular by the stagnation of a whilom subversive class  Suvin ix

In the first case, estrangement is a creative approach ... for exploring the novum, but in the second case it is an opium for the people  Suvin ix

SF as the *literature of cognitive estrangement*  Suvin 4

this genre has always been wedded to a hope of finding in the unknown the ideal environment, tribe, state, intelligence, or other aspect of the Supreme Good (or to a fear of and revulsion from its contrary)  Suvin 5

*SF is, then, a literary genre whose necessary and sufficient conditions are the presence and interaction of estrangement and cogni(8)tion, and whose main formal device is an imaginative framework alternative to the author's empirical environment*  Suvin 7-8

the symbiosis of popular science and juvenile adventure finds it impossible to mimic SF without regressing into their homologue of the *fairy tale*, with its victorious hero, foiled villain, damsel in distress, and quaint helpers or marvelous helping objects. Such sub-Vernean or Grensbackian SF does not change the fairy tale structure but only the motivation of its devices: it pretends to explain away the supernatural by reassigning it to natural science and noble scientists  Suvin 23

a hyping-up of the old grape juice into the new wine  Suvin 24

Utopia is the verbal construction of a particular quasihuman community where sociopolitical institutions, norms, and individual relationships are organised according to a more perfect principle than in the author's community, this construction being based on estrangement arising out of an alternative historical hypothesis  Suvin 49

utopia is always aimed at human relations, but uts characters do not have to be human or even outwardly anthropomorphic  Suvin 60

*SF is distinguished by the narrative dominance or hegemony of a fictional "novum" (novelty, innovation) validated by cognitive logic*  Suvin 63

anti-scientific SF is just as much within the scientific horizon (namely a misguided reaction to repressive—capitalist or bureaucratic—abuse of science) as, say, literary utopia and anti-utopia both are within the perfectibilist horizon  Suvin 67

the necessary correlate of the novum is an *alternate reality*, one that posesses a *different historical time* corresponding to different human relationships and sociocultural norms actualised by the narration  Suvin 71

though mutants or Martians, ants or intelligent nautiloids can be used as signifiers, they can only signify human relationships, given that we cannot—at least so far—imagine other ones  Suvin 71

the cognitive value of all SF, including anticipation-tales, is to be found in its analogical reference to the author's present rather than in predictions, discrete or global  Suvin 78

all space operas can be translated back into the Social Darwinism of the Westerns and similar adventure-tales by substituting colts for ray-guns and Indians for the slimy monsters of Betelgeuse  Suvin 82
