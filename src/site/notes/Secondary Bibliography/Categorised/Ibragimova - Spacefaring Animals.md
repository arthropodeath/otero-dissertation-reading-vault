The initial premise of relations between the human and spacefaring animal is rooted in the anthropocentric paradigm, prioritizing humans’ needs and desires and casting the animal other as less agential, less sentient, and less deserving of respect. This premise covers all spacefaring animals regardless of their origin—whether they evolve independently in alien worlds or are designed by humans in laboratories using either terrestrial or alien materials. The exploitative and extractive practices applied to the spacefaring animal correspond to the practices applied to the nonhuman animal in the real world, which makes their analysis relevant to exposing the oppression of the nonhuman animal through the estranging perspective of alien animals and laboratory-designed species (Ibragimova 155

The spacefaring animal creates an environment for humans during spaceflight, which extends the parallel with the real nonhuman animal to the entire nonhuman world, without which humans cannot exist and whose contribution to human life cannot be estimated or scaled. The tension inherent in humans’ disregard for the complicated process of entangled existence explodes in multiple narratives of spacefaring animals going rogue and forcefully claiming their agency. (Ibragimova 156

The initial subjugated position of the spacefaring animal renders it devoid of agency, pliable in the hands of the dominant species—either as a tool of exploitation or a material to be extracted, utilized, modified, and eventually disposed of. However, it also germinates the seeds of resistance, the ways of reclaiming freedom, reversing the hierarchies, and challenging the existing status quo. (Ibragimova 158

The spacefaring animal as an open multi-species entity possesses the generative and innovative quality with its porous boundaries and inherent openness for cooperation, co-habitation, and co-evolution. The nonhuman animal’s body performs a function of a domestic space for passengers during spaceflights, creating the ultimate intimacy between the human and the animal. (Ibragimova 158

Questioning the hierarchies and laying bare the dependence of humans on the nonhuman environment, the bodily connection humans form with spacefaring animals paves the way for companionship between species. (Ibragimova 159
