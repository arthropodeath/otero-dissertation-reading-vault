, the simultaneity and indeterminacy at the core of postmodern thinking make it indispensable in contemporary debates on the dichotomy between human and non-human entities, especially as artificial intelligence and robots become increasingly efficient partners and rivals in our classrooms and workplaces. While robot slavery has been introduced as a resolution to the binary opposition between humans and non-humans, postmodernism reminds us that this remedy is contentious and not new 293

The simultaneity of differences is the simulacrum at the center of postmodern thinking. As such, I reveal how it underwrites the imperatives inherent in the discursive processes that support human sentience while also constraining the advancement of artificial intelligence (AI) in the form of machine learning 294
