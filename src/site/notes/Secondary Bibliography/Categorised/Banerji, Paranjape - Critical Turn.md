About [[Terminology/Posthumanism\|Posthumanism]], [[Terminology/Post-Colonial Studies\|Post-Colonial Studies]]

**the postcolonial and subaltern condition becomes assimilated into a posthumanism with or without consent, just as the postmodern needs to contend with the ethics of a radical anthropological alterity surpassing historical difference. Banerji, Paranjape 1**

If transhumanism and posthumanism have an uneasy relationship, postcolonialism, though closer to posthumanism, has its difficulties with both discourses. The problematic nature of these relations arises from the loci of the human and the technological in all three Banerji, Paranjape 5

**Though a critical posthumanism could be seen as liberating to postcolonialism from the viewpoint of its critique of universalist, post-Enlightenment humanism and the triumphalist myth of technological progress and colonial subjugation, it fails to account for subalternity within the discourse of its nonhuman others. Must the subaltern be accepted as ‘human’ within a global anthropocene ecology before he or she can become posthuman? Banerji, Paranjape 5**
