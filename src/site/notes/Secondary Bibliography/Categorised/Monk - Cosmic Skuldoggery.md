**If space opera is a subgenre, it is a subgenre that is entitled, at the very least, to its own critical vocabulary and standards. The effective way to proceed with a discussion of space opera is to abandon definition on the grounds that space opera is not susceptible of definition, being neither static nor monolithic. It is not a collection of texts but an attitudinal bias, and its literary expression reveals an authorial mindset which sees the extraterrestrial universe in holistic terms as both knowable and manageable, just as the writers of the models from which it derives had seen the terrestrial world. The space opera bias is betrayed by the monomythic romance mode, action-adventure plot, emphatic closure, optimism, social naivete, mimeticism (in Frye's sense of the term), mix of entertainment and instruction, maximal encoding, fudge factors, minimal proleptic continuity, conflict (human/ human or alien/human) in both stories and subsequently novels. (Monk 300**

**Like the action-adventure story, space opera is essentially a romance (using the term very loosely) narrative rather than a realistic or naturalistic narrative. It employs the myth of the hero (the heroic monomyth) (Monk 304**

**the protagonist (in early science fiction always male, but more recently as often female) goes out into space and encounters its wonders and dangers (including, more often than not, aliens, whether friendly or hostile), finds him/herself in great dangers of bizarre kinds, and finally emerges triumphant having won some form of reward (the survival of his/her ship or of Earth) (Monk 305**

**Moreover, like other romance forms, space opera is generally optimistic (Monk 305**

Humanity's ability to deal successfully with aliens in a variety of situations features prominently and emphatically over and over again in space opera. Space opera is also a naive form, in that it rarely examines its assumptions beyond the obvious (Monk 306

Space opera, again like other science fiction and other romance forms, both entertains and teaches. Unlike some so-called "hard" science fiction, however, the balance in space opera is in favor of entertainment. (Monk 308

**Space opera is highly encoded, making extensive use of conventions and tropes. (Monk 309**

Space opera ignores, for the most part, proleptic continuity. There is very. little in the way of backfill for the story's present: the reader is dumped into the near or distant future, with no more than a casual backward glance at how the cosmos got to be the way it is (Monk 309

Typically, without offering serious or detailed analysis of the background of the multiplicity, space opera introduces multispecies galaxies in which species meet without a metaphysical fuss, moral qualms, or a great deal of introspection-much as different cultures encounter each other in any large city today-while leaving room for a good deal of variation in the individual encounters.
